Particulate Nature of Matter

Anything that occupies space and has mass is known as matter. Everything around us is a form or example of matter. For example, huge buildings, bridges, electrons, DNA, air and even the ground we walk on. they all qualify as matter. A matter is said to be composed of particles which are basically atoms and molecules.

Four Main States of Matter

The Solid, Liquid, Gas, and Plasma.

Particle Theory of Matter

It describes the microscopic properties of atoms and their interactions, which result in observable macroscopic properties (such as pressure, volume, and temperature). This theory can be used to explain why matter exists in different phases, as well as how matter can change from one phase to the next.

Changes of State

A change of state (also called phase change), is a physical change from one state of matter to another. for example: from solid to liquid, liquid to gasses or solid to gasses.

States of Matter

Each state of matter has unique properties based on the arrangement and behavior of its particles.
